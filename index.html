INSPIRE

1. Gauge Theories and High-Energy Physics

My work begins with the geometry of gauge fields: connections on principal bundles, symmetry reduction, and the role of fibre bundles in classical and quantum field theory. I am particularly interested in the interplay between gauge invariance, constraint surfaces, and the structure of conserved quantities.

2. Quantum Simulation

I explore how gauge and gravitational systems can be simulated on noisy quantum devices. My focus is on hybrid quantum–classical pipelines, error mitigation strategies, and the implementation of geometric constraints in controllable experimental settings. This connects the abstract structure of field theory with realizations in atomic, optical, and superconducting platforms.

3. Covariant Phase Space with Boundaries

I study the geometric formulation of field theories on manifolds with boundary, focusing on the covariant phase space and its symplectic structure. This includes questions about surface charges, asymptotic symmetries, and the boundary degrees of freedom that appear in gauge theories.
